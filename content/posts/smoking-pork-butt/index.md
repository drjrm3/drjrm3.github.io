## Context

My pork butt low and slow method involves cooking it pretty much overnight and having it be ready for dinner
at around 5 the next day. I will give timelines for what to do at each step including timeline to be
ready by around 5 that day.

I will keep this entry updated as I fine tune my methods. I did it one night with a long (very long)
chat with Gemini.

This post is purely a "hey - I did this one time and it was good - let me make some notes on it". I will come back
at a later time and give more detail.

## Required tools

* Traeger Timberline
* Traeger Apple Pellets
* Traeger Pork and Poultry Rub

## The Cut

I get a 8 Pound Pork Butt (bone in) at Publix.

## Prep

To prep this - I cut off the major pieces of fat, and put on an entire bottle of the rub.

Putting on the rub and then putting it back in the fridge for a while (up to 12 hours) helps build a good bark.
More time means more flavor penetration into the surface and a better bark.
The salt in the rub will also have more time to draw out moisture and then get reabsorbed,
which seasons the meat a bit deeper.

## The Cook

The cook follows these general timelines:

* Day 1:

11:00 am: On the day you will start the smoke, buy the pork butt
02:00 pm: Put on the rub, wrap in saran wrap, putt back in fridge
10:00 pm: Preheat the Traeger to 225
11:00 pm: Pull pork butt out of fridge, put on Traeger

* Day 2:

07:00 am: Temp should be around 150
08:00 am: Temp should be between 150 and 160. Wrap in butcher paper, put back on, crank temp to 250°F
02:30 pm: Temp should hit 203. Pull off the grill, put in YETI
05:00 pm: Pull out of cooler, shred for dinner.

### Getting It On

At around 11 pm I fire up the Traeger and bring it to a temp of 225. Once that is at 225 I put on the pork butt
on the top shelf.

Once that is on and things look good. I'll go to sleep.

### The Stall

I wake up around 7 or 8 the next day and it should be close to the stall temp 165 I will wrap it in butcher paper.
I put it back on (restick with probes) and put it back on.

Note that "the stall" is primarily a plateau that typically starts around 150–155°F and can last until
the meat pushes through to around 170°F. What's happening is evaporative cooling on the meat's surface
balancing out the heat from the smoker, so the internal temp just flatlines for hours.

The "stall" temp can be anywhere from 150 to 165. I increase the temp at that point to 250°F

### Pushing Through

Once it hits the 203 temperature (should be around 2 pm) I will pull it off. 

## The Rest

By about 2 pm, it should be at the 203 temperature. At that point, I take it off (carefully) - keep it
wrapped in the butcher paper, and put it in my YETI cooler and *pack* it with towels.

If I pull it off at 2 pm and put it in the YETI I try to aim for about 2.5 hours in the YETI. The YETI
can keep it hot for up to 4 hours, so pulling it off sooner is not a problem. It's good to let it rest for at
least an hour though.

## Pulling and Serving

When I finally take it out at around 5 pm I pull it and serve. If done correctly, the bone should just fall off!
